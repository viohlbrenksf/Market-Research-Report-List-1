<p><p>The electrolytic caustic soda market is primarily segmented into three production processes: the Mercury Process, Diaphragm Process, and Ion-Exchange Membrane Process. The Mercury Process employs mercury cells to produce caustic soda, though it raises environmental concerns. The Diaphragm Process uses a porous diaphragm to separate chlorine and caustic soda, offering moderate efficiency and safety. The Ion-Exchange Membrane Process is the most advanced, utilizing selective membranes for high purity and energy efficiency, making it increasingly favored in modern production methods.</p></p>
