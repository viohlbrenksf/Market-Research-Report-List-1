<p><p>Crash Test Barriers play a crucial role in assessing vehicle safety during impact tests. The Frontal Impact Barrier is designed to simulate head-on collisions, evaluating how vehicles absorb energy and protect occupants. In contrast, the Side Impact Barrier tests a vehicle's defense against lateral collisions, focusing on the protection of passengers from side impacts. Both types of barriers are essential for ensuring compliance with safety standards, guiding automotive manufacturers in designing safer vehicles for consumers.</p></p>
