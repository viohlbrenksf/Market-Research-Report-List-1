<p><p>The 20% glass filled nylon market comprises two primary manufacturing types: injection molding and extrusion molding. Injection molding involves forcing molten nylon mixed with 20% glass fibers into a mold to create intricate shapes, offering high precision and efficiency for complex components. In contrast, extrusion molding shapes the material into continuous profiles or sheets, ideal for products requiring uniform cross-sections. Both processes enhance the mechanical properties of nylon, making it suitable for applications in automotive, industrial, and consumer goods sectors.</p></p>
