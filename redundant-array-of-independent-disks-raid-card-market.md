<p><p>The RAID card market consists of various types that cater to different storage protocols. SAS (Serial Attached SCSI) and SATA (Serial ATA) are commonly used for traditional hard drives and offer robust data protection and redundancy. NVMe (Non-Volatile Memory Express) is designed for high-speed flash storage, delivering superior performance through low latency. Each type serves distinct needs in data management, with SAS/SATA focusing on reliability and cost-effectiveness, while NVMe targets high-performance applications requiring rapid data access.</p></p>
